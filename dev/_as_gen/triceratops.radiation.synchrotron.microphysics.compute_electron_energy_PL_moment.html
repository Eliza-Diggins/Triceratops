triceratops.radiation.synchrotron.microphysics.compute_electron_energy_PL_moment#

triceratops.radiation.synchrotron.microphysics.compute_electron_energy_PL_moment(p: _ArrayLike, E_min: _UnitBearingArrayLike = <Quantity 8.18710578e-07 erg>, E_max: _UnitBearingArrayLike = <Quantity inf erg>, *, order: int = 1) → _ArrayLike[source]#

Compute the order-th moment of a power-law electron energy distribution.

This evaluates

\[\int_{E_{\min}}^{E_{\max}} E^{\,\mathrm{order} - p}\, dE.\]
Parameters:

  • p (float or array-like) – Power-law index of the electron energy distribution.

  • E_min (float, array-like, or astropy.units.Quantity) – Minimum electron energy (must be > 0). Default is the electron rest-mass energy.

  • E_max (float, array-like, or astropy.units.Quantity) – Maximum electron energy. May be inf if the integral converges. Default is inf.

  • order (int, optional) – Moment order. Default is 1 (energy moment).

Returns:

Moment value(s).

Return type:

float or numpy.ndarray

Notes

This is a wrapper of the lower-level gamma-moment function. It converts energies to Lorentz factors before passing them off to the gamma-moment function.

To perform this calculation, we use

\[M^{(l)}_E = \int_{E_{\min}}^{E_{\max}} E^{l - p} \, dE = \int_{\gamma_{\min}}^{\gamma_{\max}} (m_e c^2 \gamma)^{l - p} m_e c^2 \, d\gamma = (m_e c^2)^{l + 1 - p} \int_{\gamma_{\min}}^{\gamma_{\max}} \gamma^{l - p} \, d\gamma.\]

and then simply compute the moment in gamma-space, multiplying by the appropriate factor of \((m_e c^2)^{\mathrm{order} + 1 - p}\) to account for the change of variables.
